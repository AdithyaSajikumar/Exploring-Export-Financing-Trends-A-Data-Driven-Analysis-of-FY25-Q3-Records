# Exploring Export Financing Trends: A Data-Driven Analysis of FY25 Q3 Records

## üìë Overview
This project analyzes the FY25 Q3 export financing dataset from the U.S. Export-Import Bank (Data.gov).  
The goal is to clean, explore, and visualize the data to uncover key insights such as:
- Approval trends
- Country-level financing
- Product demand
- Program comparisons
- Support for diverse businesses
- Seasonal patterns
- Financial risk exposure

By turning raw records into structured analysis, the project shows how finance and banking analytics can guide smarter decision-making in international trade.

---

## üìÇ Dataset
- **Source:** [U.S. Export-Import Bank (Data.gov)](https://img.exim.gov/s3fs-public/dataset/vbhv-d8am/Data.Gov_-_FY25_Q3.csv)

- **Attributes:** Loan approvals, disbursements, exposures, interest rates, program details, country, product, and business support categories.

### Step 4: Insights & Recommendations
- **Insight #1:** U.S. dominates financing ‚Üí diversify globally  
- **Insight #2:** Small businesses supported, but inclusivity gaps remain ‚Üí create targeted programs  
- **Insight #3:** Technology and industry exports drive growth ‚Üí strengthen these sectors  
- **Insight #4:** Mid-term loans riskier ‚Üí adjust risk models  
- **Insight #5:** Seasonal demand patterns ‚Üí align programs with cycles  
- **Insight #6:** Program effectiveness varies ‚Üí focus on high-performing programs  

**Business Recommendations:**  
- Expand global financing  
- Support women & minority-owned businesses  
- Focus on technology/industry exports  
- Manage loan risks better  
- Plan for seasonal demand
